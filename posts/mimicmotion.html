Video Generation

MimicMotion: High-Quality Human Motion Video Generation with Confidence-Aware Pose Guidance

March 5, 2026 · Zhang et al. (ICML 2025)

What Does MimicMotion Do?

MimicMotion takes a still photo of a person and makes them move. You give it a reference image and a video of someone else moving. It copies the body poses from that video and applies them to the person in the photo.

The goals are simple: copy movements from one video to a photo, keep the person looking the same, make the motion look smooth and natural, and do it all without needing 3D tools.

Background

DWPose: Finding Body Positions

DWPose detects 133 body points — including body, feet, face, and hands. Older tools like OpenPose were either slow or missed small details like fingers. DWPose uses a small fast model that was trained to copy the results of a larger slow model, so it's both fast and accurate.

AnimateAnyone

An earlier system for pose-guided video. It works but has problems: the video flickers and the background is unstable.

Stable Video Diffusion (SVD)

SVD turns a single image into a short 3-4 second video using diffusion. It generates realistic motion but has no way to control the pose.

What MimicMotion Adds

1. Making Long Videos from Short Clips

The model trains on 16-frame clips, so it can only make short videos at first. To make longer ones, MimicMotion splits the video into overlapping pieces and blends them together.

The key idea: frames in the middle of each piece get more weight than frames at the edges. Why? Middle frames can look at frames both before and after them. Edge frames can only look in one direction, so they're less reliable.

2. Using Pose Confidence

DWPose gives each body point a confidence score. A hand detected with 99% confidence is very different from one at 31%, but normally the model treats them the same.

MimicMotion feeds the confidence score directly into the network:

  • High confidence (0.99): "Put a hand here for sure"
  • Low confidence (0.35): "Maybe a hand is here, but don't force it"

This stops bad pose guesses from creating weird artifacts in the video.

3. Better Hands

Hands are small — about 10% of the image — so the model normally doesn't pay much attention to them. MimicMotion fixes this by multiplying the hand loss by 10× during training, forcing the model to care more about getting hands right.

Training Details

  • Data: 4,436 dancing videos from the internet
  • Base model: Stable Video Diffusion
  • Hardware: 8 A100 GPUs, 20 rounds of training

Summary

MimicMotion makes pose-guided video smoother and longer than previous methods. It uses confidence scores to avoid bad poses, gives extra attention to hands, and blends overlapping clips to create long videos without flickering.
