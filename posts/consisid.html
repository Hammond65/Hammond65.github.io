Video Generation

ConsisID: Identity-Preserving Text-to-Video Generation by Frequency Decomposition

February 20, 2026 · Yuan et al. (CVPR 2025)

The Problem

When you generate a video from a person's photo and a text description, the face often changes throughout the video. The person in frame 1 might not look like the same person in frame 20. ConsisID tries to keep the face consistent.

Why Is This Hard?

Older methods used U-Net networks, which have skip connections that naturally keep details at different scales. Newer models use DiT (Diffusion Transformer), which is better overall but doesn't have these built-in detail-preserving connections. So keeping a face looking the same is harder with DiT.

Two Types of Face Information

  1. Low-frequency: The overall face shape, proportions, and outline. These are the big, smooth features.
  2. High-frequency: The fine details — eye texture, lip shape, skin patterns, wrinkles. These are what make a face unique.

Transformers are not great at picking up high-frequency details on their own.

How ConsisID Works

ConsisID is built on CogVideoX-5B (a DiT-based video model). Its main idea: split face identity into low-frequency and high-frequency parts, and feed each one into the model differently.

Global Extractor (Big Features)

The reference face photo is passed through a VAE encoder and combined with the noise input. The VAE naturally smooths out fine details, keeping only the large-scale face structure. This helps the model learn the basic face shape quickly.

Local Extractor (Fine Details)

For the unique identity features — things that stay the same no matter the expression or angle — ConsisID uses two sources:

  • ArcFace: A face recognition model that outputs 512 numbers capturing identity
  • CLIP: A vision model that captures broader visual meaning

These two are combined using a Q-Former module, which merges them into one set of identity features. These are then fed into the DiT blocks during video generation.

How Others Approached This

  • DreamBooth: Fine-tunes the whole model for each person. Works well but takes over an hour per person.
  • LoRA: Only trains a small part of the model. Faster, but still needs per-person training.
  • IP-Adapter / InstantID: Work without per-person training, but only for images, not video.
  • ID-Animator: Works for video, but only with U-Net models.

ConsisID is the first to do this for DiT-based video models without needing to train for each person.

Summary

ConsisID keeps faces consistent in generated videos by splitting identity into big features and fine details, then feeding each into the model the right way. It works with modern DiT models and doesn't need any per-person fine-tuning.
